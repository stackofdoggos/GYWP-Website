What community sports became when nobody had to leave

In the years after humanity stopped dying, the games did not stop — they only grew longer, stranger, and more local. Town leagues became county leagues, then regional grids that crossed rivers and mountain ranges. The probes Nine, Ten, and Juice still watch from orbit, calling plays the way weather satellites once called storms.

This is not the normal-era NFL on the home scoreboard. This is what happened when every Friday night lights became a constellation.

I'm a huge speculative fiction fan, and one of my favorite pieces in that genre is Jon Bois' 17776 and its sequal, 20020. Set in the far far future, both of Bois' 5-numbered titles take place on an Earth where humans cannot be born or killed. In this way, Bois explore themes of existentialism, despair, hope, football, the cosmos, and the meaning of life. Wait, football? See at heart, Bois is a sports writer, and he cleverly uses that to his advantage to craft two compelling narratives.

The idea is that with infinite time on their hands, humans had

> “a choice. They could continue wandering through the endless darkness, an absence of everything they loved, an endless void of disappointment and loneliness ...
> 
> ... or they could look down, and embrace what they always had and loved.”

And so, humans decided to look down. To look down and play football. Endless, contorted games of football played over thousands of miles. Games with endless sets of rules that took millennia of people-hours to edit, and games with nearly none. The football played is just wacky enough to be entertaining, yet trivial enough to allow our narrators (3 sentient space probes) to explore the themes mentioned above. 

This site is an homage to those works and I suggest you go check them out!!
